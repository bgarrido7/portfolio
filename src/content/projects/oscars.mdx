## About

Watching the Oscars has become a yearly ritual for me.

Once the nominees are announced, I go through everything carefully and try to watch as many of the nominated films as I can before the ceremony. Then I print out the official Oscars ballot, sit down, and start filling in my predictions category by category.

There’s something about having it on paper that makes it more real—like I’m officially locking in my choices for the year.

I’ve built up a small collection of these papers over time. Each one captures that specific year and who I thought would win. Looking back on them has become part of the experience.

But what really keeps me coming back is how it makes the Oscars night itself more exciting. Having my own predictions sitting next to me during the ceremony turns every announcement into something personal. Every win is either a hit or a miss for me, and it makes watching feel a lot more engaging than just following along.

So I built this app.

It’s a way to immortalize my predictions and the Oscar wins year after year, so that even if one day I inevitably lose those paper ballots, the history of what I chose and what actually won is still preserved in one place.
